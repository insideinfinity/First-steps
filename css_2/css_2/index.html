The world's first car


The Benz Patent-Motorwagen was the world's first car with an internal combustion engine (like the Daimler car), built in 1885 by the German inventor Carl Benz.


Karl Friedrich Michael Benz


In his workshop, Karl Benz began to develop new internal combustion engines. On December 31, 1878, he received a patent for a two-stroke gasoline engine. Karl Benz soon patented all the important components and systems of the future automobile: gas pedal, battery-operated ignition system and spark plug, carburetor, clutch, gearbox and water-cooled radiator.
